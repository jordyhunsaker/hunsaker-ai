Not every company is ready for AI. Before investing significant resources, it's worth honestly assessing where you stand. Here's a framework I use with clients to evaluate AI readiness.

## 1. Data Readiness

AI systems are only as good as the data they're trained on. Ask yourself:

**Do you have the data you need?** Many AI projects fail not because of technical challenges, but because the necessary data doesn't exist or isn't accessible.

**Is your data clean?** Inconsistencies, duplicates, and gaps will all surface in your AI outputs. Budget time for data cleanup.

**Is your data accessible?** Data locked in spreadsheets, legacy systems, or people's heads can't power AI. You need programmatic access.

**Score yourself:**
- 0-1: Data is scattered and inconsistent
- 2-3: Some centralization, known quality issues
- 4-5: Clean, centralized, accessible data

## 2. Process Readiness

AI augments or automates existing processes. You need to understand those processes first.

**Are your processes documented?** You can't automate what you can't describe. Start by mapping current workflows.

**Are they consistent?** If ten people do the same task ten different ways, AI will struggle. Standardization comes first.

**Where's the repetition?** AI excels at handling high-volume, repetitive tasks. Identify where your team spends time on work that could be automated.

**Score yourself:**
- 0-1: Ad-hoc processes, high variation
- 2-3: Some documentation, inconsistent execution
- 4-5: Well-documented, standardized processes

## 3. Team Readiness

Your team will be building, maintaining, and working alongside AI systems.

**Do you have technical talent?** You don't need a machine learning team, but you need people who can work with APIs, data, and software systems.

**Is there appetite for change?** AI will change how people work. Resistance will slow or kill projects. Gauge enthusiasm early.

**Who will own this?** Every AI initiative needs a champion who will drive it forward through obstacles.

**Score yourself:**
- 0-1: Limited technical capability, change-resistant culture
- 2-3: Some technical talent, mixed enthusiasm
- 4-5: Strong technical team, culture of innovation

## 4. Infrastructure Readiness

AI systems need to run somewhere and integrate with your existing tools.

**What's your cloud situation?** Modern AI typically runs in the cloud. If you're still on-premise for everything, that's a hurdle.

**Can your systems talk to each other?** APIs, webhooks, and integrations are the plumbing of AI. Assess your current connectivity.

**What about security and compliance?** AI systems handle data. Understand your requirements before you start.

**Score yourself:**
- 0-1: On-premise, siloed systems
- 2-3: Partial cloud adoption, some integrations
- 4-5: Cloud-native, well-integrated systems

## 5. Leadership Readiness

Without executive support, AI projects become science projects.

**Is there budget?** AI implementation isn't free. Ensure there's real financial commitment.

**Is there patience?** AI projects take time to show ROI. Leaders need to understand this isn't a quick fix.

**Is there a business case?** The best AI projects start with clear business objectives, not technology curiosity.

**Score yourself:**
- 0-1: No budget or executive interest
- 2-3: Some interest, unclear commitment
- 4-5: Strong executive sponsor with budget

## Your Total Score

Add up your scores across all five dimensions:

**20-25: AI Ready.** You have strong foundations. Focus on identifying high-impact use cases and move quickly.

**15-19: Almost There.** Address specific gaps before major investments. Consider starting with a pilot project.

**10-14: Building Foundations.** Significant work needed before AI will succeed. Focus on data and process improvements first.

**Below 10: Start with Basics.** AI isn't the priority right now. Build foundational capabilities first.
